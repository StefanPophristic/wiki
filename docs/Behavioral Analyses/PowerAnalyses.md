# Power Analyses
- [Vasishth (2022): Recommended by Ailis Cournane](https://www.annualreviews.org/content/journals/10.1146/annurev-linguistics-031220-010345)
- [Kumle et al. (2021): Recommended as a good intro by Meredith Tamminga](https://link.springer.com/article/10.3758/s13428-021-01546-0)
  - Meredith recommends using the `mixedpower` R package from this paper if this is your first time doing power analyses, as it is much more straight forward and usable out of the box
- [Brysbaert (2019): Recommended as supplemental reading to Kumle et al (2021), but note that it is much more technical](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6640316/)
- [Brysbaert & Stevens (2018): Recommended as supplemental reading to Kumle et al (2021), but note that it is much more technical](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6646942/)
- [Matuschek et al. (2017): Recommended as supplemental reading to Kumle et al (2021), but note that it is much more technical](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0749596X17300013)
- [Chris Jungerius Online tutorial](https://cjungerius.github.io/powersim/)
